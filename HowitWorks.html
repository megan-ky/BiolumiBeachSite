The Science of Bioluminescence

Bioluminescence is a natural phenomenon where organisms emit light through a chemical reaction. It involves luciferin (a light-producing molecule) and luciferase (an enzyme), which react with oxygen to release energy as visible light.

iStock

Organisms That Glow

Bioluminescence is found in many marine species like plankton, jellyfish, and certain types of fish. On land, fireflies and fungi such as glowing mushrooms display this magical light.

iStock

Practical Uses in Nature

  • Attraction: Some organisms use light to attract mates or prey.
  • Camouflage: Certain species emit light to blend with their surroundings.
  • Communication: Bioluminescence helps species send signals to others.
  • Defense: Light is used to scare predators or confuse them.
